Overview

What does it take to build trust in a platform that promises to save you tens of thousands of dollars?

That’s the question that guided our team as we took on the challenge of redesigning Bramble’s desktop website. As a new real estate brokerage with bold claims and cutting-edge technology, Bramble needs an interface that not only reflects its innovation but also instills confidence in buyers and sellers navigating one of the most important decisions of their lives.

Problem

Unclear Business Model

The original interface lacked clear communication of Bramble’s unique AI features and core benefits, like the flat-fee rebate. Users were unfamiliar with Bramble’s nontraditional model.

Establishing Trust

Selling or buying a home is a big deal. Users needed to feel confident using a platform leveraging AI to disrupt the traditional model.

Explaining Innovation

Our challenge was to design an interface that conveyed credibility, explained new technology intuitively, and supported users unfamiliar with Bramble’s approach.

Design Process

To solve these problems, we each created independent interface sketches and then collaboratively critiqued our sketches to identify essential features.

Independent Sketches

To begin our design process, each member of our team brainstormed a distinct design for Bramble’s desktop interface. Each set of sketches focused on exploring different layouts, visual hierarchies, and flows for the core user actions we established in the form of a home page, explore page, and FAQ page! Collaborative Design Critique: Determining Essential Features

Across Pages

  • Create emotional and warmth appeal through visuals & illustrations
  • Clearly communicate Bramble’s value through savings highlights & testimonials
  • Minimalist nav bar to emphasize visuals

Explore Page

  • Reduces decision fatigue & supports varied user flows through a streamlined property type selector
  • Simplifies complex searches with a clear, scrollable filter panel
  • Enables quick comparison with uniform, detail-rich listing cards

Home Page

  • Provide intuitive access to Bramble’s AI-powered search via central search bar

FAQ Page

  • Visually guide users through a sectioned FAQ
  • Make content organized & accessible through accordion layout

Solution V1: Wireframe

After generating four distinct design concepts, we combined the strongest elements from each to create a cohesive, user-centered desktop interface for Bramble. Our final wireframes reflect intentional decisions drawn from our diverse sketches, balancing clarity, trust, and innovation.

Incorporating Feedback

We received the following feedback from user interaction teaching assistants at Brown.

Scrollable Homepage

Feedback led to a redesign of the homepage to be scrollable, with distinct sections for brand messaging, the search bar, and key differentiators.

Clarified AI Integration

A section explaining Bramble’s AI workflows was added to the homepage to clarify its role in the home-buying process.

Improved Interactivity

An expand button was added to house cards to make them clearly clickable.

Visual Balance

While visuals were recommended for the how-to guide, we opted for clear text to avoid overcrowding the section.

Positive Perception

Plans for including the About and Sell pages in the high-fidelity prototype received positive feedback.

Solution V2: High Fidelity Prototype

HiFi Protype Feedback & CEO Demo

We are very thankful to Bramble's CEO Daniel Hsu, who provided feedback on our mockup. His feedback includes:

  • The CEO suggested using a different secondary color, as Bramble berry doesn’t work well for backgrounds and should be used sparingly. We felt like the colors went well together but have liked to experiment with different color schema if we had more time.
  • He recommended clarifying the search bar functionality to better guide users on how to use it and highlight the three search queries offered by Bramble: by address, by link and by natural language search.
  • He noted that some parts of the UI, like filters and the expanded home view, could be simplified or made more visual to improve clarity and usability.
  • Additionally, he appreciated the emphasis on visuals, especially the property display screen, and encouraged exploring video integration.

User Testing

Experimental Design

“Find a home based on your personal preferences and view the full listing details of a property that interests you.”
We used this integral task to evaluate how effectively our design communicates Bramble’s purpose, conveys value, and builds trust.


Results

Search bar issues

Users repeatedly tried to interact with an inactive search bar, leading to confusion and frustration.

AI functionality unclear

Even with the added section on Bramble’s AI workflows, users struggled to identify where AI was used and suggested more increased transparency through explanations in key touchpoints.

Aesthetic

Users appreciated the aesthetic and user-friendly layout.

Successful Task Completion

All successfully completed the task of navigating to and viewing house details.

Reflection

Throughout our iterative design process, our team learned the importance of balancing user needs with business goals and continuously improving based on feedback. Let quickly highlight a few key parts of this process:

  1. We started with a bunch of different sketches, then combined the strongest ideas into a wireframe that aligned with Bramble’s business goals.
  2. After presenting to our TA, we made improvements like turning our home screen into a scrollable page, adding clearer visual structure, and making elements like listing cards more obviously clickable.
  3. We recognized a recurring challenge as users struggled to understand how AI was integrated into the company. So we reached out to the CEO and learned more about the AI features. Now that we were better informed we decided to include a section explaining Bramble’s AI workflows on the landing page.
  4. User testing ensured the functionality of our hifi prototype and allowed us to see the interactions of those not familiar with the class or project!
  5. We then reached out to the CEO again to share our final prototype and get his feedback. Although we did not have the time to implement the changes he suggested we were satisfied with the positive comments he made and that he felt like we accurately conveyed his mission and business goals.

Overall, we learned to appreciate the collaboration and back and forth nature of designing a functional application. If we had more time, we would focus on making the product more interactive. This project taught us a lot about designing with users in mind and being open to change along the way to increase the memorability, learnability and efficiency of an application.
